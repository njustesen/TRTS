Turn Sequence

Turn Order
Each turn starts with the Macro Phase that is done secretly and simultaneously, followed by the Micro Phase, that is executed one player at the time in the current turn order.

Macro Phase
  1. Income
  2. Deployment
  3. Move & Produce
Micro Phase
In the current turn order, players can either make a Push or pass. When all players passes in a round, the Micro Phase ends and a new turn starts.
Income
First turn: receive one mineral and proceed to the Move & Produce step.

All following turns: each worker can collect either one Mineral or one Gas (if they did not attack last turn) from areas where you own a resource gathering facility.

No more minerals can be gathered from one area than the number of mineral fields in it. At most three gas can be gathered from each gas extractor you own.
Deployment
Units, structures, and upgrades that were produced in last turn's Production Phase are now deployed.

Some things take more that one turn to produce (look for the symbol). In such case, use counters remember how many turns it has been under production.
Move & Produce

Move
You can secretly move all your units on your Minimap from, through, and to areas you control. Look at the Speed value to see how far things can move during a turn. Use counters to remember how far things have moved as they can move again the Micro Phase if they have movement points left.

Produce
Workers with the Warp, Construct, or Morph ability can use them to produce structures. They can move before they produce and Probes are also allowed to move after.

Structures can train units, produce add-ons, research upgrades, or morph into a higher tier as shown in their rules. Each structure can only do one of these actions at a time, except for Zerg structures with Larva (find the rules on the Race sheet).

Gas Extractors can only be build on top of an unoccupied gas geyser.

Anything in production can at any time be cancelled. Simply remove or revert it and claim the resources that you paid.

Structures in productions have half (rounded down) of their Health and Shield until they are deployed. Units being morphed are in an Egg with 10 HP. Archons being summoned have full Health and Shield.

Supply
Producing units requires some amount of supplies which is indicated in by on your race sheet. Some units and buildings give you supply, which is also indicated on the race sheet. The total supply cost of the units in production cannot be higher than the number of supplies you have.

Area Control
You control areas that only have your control marker on it.

Areas with more than one control marker on are contested.

An area is reachable, for a unit with enough movement points left to move there, moving only through connected areas under control, following all terrain rules.
Push
To make a push, gather a Control Group of units on your Minimap that can all reach the same area. The area must have an enemy control marker and be an area you contest or an area connected to another area you control. Units must be able to reach the target area by only passing through areas that you control. If some units are in a contested area, you have to start the push in that area. Now, declare to the other players the area on the Map that you engage. You can only make a push to the same area once per turn.

All other players now have to declare whether they are in that area.

If the area is unoccupied, remove any control marker on that area and place one of your own. Move your units to that area on your Minimap and then push forward or end the push.

If the area is occupied
  1. Attackers Reveal: Reveal all non-cloaked attackers by placing them on the edge of the area on the Map, showing where they came from.
  2. Defenders Retreat: Any of the defending players can, in the current turn order, decide to retreat with any units to any reachable area under control but not through the areas that the attackers engages from. Retreating units must be revealed unless some units or structures are left behind. If no units or buildings remain, remove any control marker on that area and place one of your own. You can now push forward or end the push.
  3. Defenders Reveal: If some units or buildings remain, the defending player must now move all units and defensive buildings to the engaged area on the Map.
  4. Attackers Retreat: The attacking player can now decide to a) retreat with all attackers to any reachable areas through the area they came from, which ends the push, or b) commit to the engagement, resulting in combat. Retreating units do not spend a Movement Point on engaging the target area.
  5. Combat: if the attackers commit, move any remaining buildings of the defending player to the area on the Map, and attackers spend a movement point moving into the area. Then perform a combat round.
Pushing Forward
Attackers that did not retreat, attack, or cast spells this turn can push forward to a new area. Form a new Control Group from your current Control Group and follow the rules above.

Ending a Push
You can also end a push, where you can move back any units in your current control group, that have not attacked or cast spells this turn, to reachable areas you control. After ending a push, it becomes the next player in the current turn order to make a push or pass.
Special Rules
Cloaked: Can only be attacked and seen by players with an observer in the area.
Detector: Can detect cloaked units.
Flying: Can only be attacked by Air Attack.
Hovers: Cannot be targeted by Spider Mines.
Swarm: Can be used at the start of every Combat Round giving them to ability to decide how any damage they are dealt is allocated while splash bonus becomes twice as effective.
Frenzy: Can attack again at the end of the combat if they survived and did attack this combat round.
Combat
Starting with units with the highest Range, also considering spells, perform a Combat Step wherein these units can attack or cast spells. In a Combat Step, first, the attacking player assigns targets and declares spells being casted, followed by the defending players in the current turn order.

All damage and spells of a Combat Step take effect simultaneously before moving on the the next Combat Step. Remove any units that are killed and use tokens to show lost health and shield points. Units can decide not to attack, or can attack in a later combat step.

Units can only attack once per turn.

Spell casters can only cast on spell with the icon per turn, unless their Energy level is upgraded.

Assign Targets
When assigning targets, split the units that will attack in this Combat Step into groups by which unit type they will target. Spell casters can declare which spells they are casting and their targets.

Air units can freely select their targets while ground units can only select targets that have the same or lower Range value. If there are no units with the same or lower Range, ground units have to select targets in ascending order of Range. Units or structures without a Range value are always targeted last unless they have used a spell in this Combat in which their Range value is equal to the lowest range of the spells.

Deal Damage
To determine the amount of damage a unit will deal to the enemy, calculate their modified attack value (MAV). The attacker can allocate the damage as they wish among the target of the same type.

MAV = Attack + Upgrades + Bonus - Armor

MAV can never be lower than 1, except for workers which always have MAV = ½. Workers thus need to attack in pairs to deal any damage.

Attack: The Attack value shown on the race sheet as Gr. if the targets are ground units or Air if they are flying.
Upgrades: The amount of bonus attack coming from researched upgrades.
Armor: The Armor value of the target units including upgrades.
Bonus: Bonus from Explosive, Concussive, Acid Spores, and Splash Damage.

Bonus
Explosive (e) attacks deal one additional damage to large units and one less to small.
Concussive (c) attacks deal an additional damage to small units and one less to large.
Acid Spore (a) attacks deal one additional attack per acid spore on the target and adds one acid spore to all targets that lose health.
Splash (s) attacks deal 1/2/3 additional damage if there are 3/7/12 or more targets of the same type.
Suicide (!) attacks kills the attacker.
Winning
Elimination
Possible ways to get eliminated:
  • You loose all units
  • You loose all structures
  • You surrender
Winning
  • All other players are eliminated
  • After turn 32, the player with the most areas under controls wins. If it's a tie, play another turn.
