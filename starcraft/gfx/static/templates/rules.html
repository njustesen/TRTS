Turn Sequence

Turn Order
Each turn starts with the Macro Phase, that is done secretly and simultaneously, followed by the Micro Phase, that is executed one player at the time in the current turn order.

Macro Phase
  1. Replenish
  2. Income
  3. Deployment
  4. Move & Produce
Micro Phase
In the current turn order, players can either make a Push or pass. When all players passes in a round, the Micro Phase ends and a new turn starts.
Replenish
Units and structures that did not participate in combat last round and start the round in a non-contested area replenish:
  1. Zerg units and structures gain full HP.
  2. Protoss units and structures gain full Shield.
  3. Spell casters gain Energy. Structures also replenish Energy in contested areas.
Income
First round: receive one mineral and proceed to the Move & Produce step.

All following rounds: each worker can collect either one Mineral or one Gas (if they were not part of combat last round) from areas where you own a resource gathering facility.

No more minerals can be gathered from one area than the number of mineral fields in it. At most three gas can be gathered from each gas extractor you own.
Deployment
Units, structures, and upgrades that were produced in last round's Production Phase are now deployed.

Some things take more that one round to produce (look for the symbol). In such case, use counters to remember how many rounds it has been under production.
Move & Produce

Move
You can secretly move all your units on your Minimap from, through, and to areas you control. Look at the Speed value to see how far things can move per round. Use counters to remember how far things have moved as they can move again the Micro Phase if they have movement points left.

Produce
Workers with the Warp, Construct, or Morph ability can use them to produce structures. They can move before they produce and Probes are also allowed to move after.

Structures can train units, produce add-ons, research upgrades, or morph into a higher tier as shown in their rules. Some units can morph into higher tier units as well. Each structure can only do one of these actions at a time, except for Zerg structures with Larva (find the rules on the Race sheet).

Gas Extractors can only be build on top of an unoccupied gas geyser.

Anything in production can at any time be cancelled. Simply remove or revert it and claim the resources that you paid.

Structures in productions have half (rounded down) of their Health and Shield until they are deployed. Units being morphed are in an Egg with 10 HP. Archons being summoned have full Health and Shield.

Supply
Producing units requires some amount of Supplies which is indicated by on your race sheet. Some units and structures give you Supply, which is also indicated on the race sheet. The total Supply cost of the units you put into production cannot be larger than your Supply.

Area Control
You control areas that only have your control marker on it.

Areas with more than one control marker are contested.

An area is reachable, for a unit with enough movement points left to move there, moving only through connected areas under control, following all terrain rules.
Push
To make a push, gather a Control Group of units on your Minimap that can all reach the same area that is controlled by an enemy or contested. Units that have retreated or been part of another push this round cannot be part of a new push. Units in a contested area have to start the push in that area. Your control group cannot start in several contested areas. Now, declare to the other players the area on the Map you engage. You can only engage to the same area once per round.

All other players now have to declare whether they are in that area.

If the area is unoccupied, remove any control markers on that area and place one of your own. Move your units to that area on your Minimap and then push forward or end the push.

If the area is occupied
  1. Attackers Reveal: Reveal all non-cloaked attackers by placing them on the edge of the area on the Map, showing where they came from.
  2. Defenders Retreat: Any of the defending players can, in the current turn order, decide to retreat with any units to any reachable area under control but not through the areas that the attackers engages from. Retreating units must be revealed unless some units or structures are left behind. If no units or structures remain, remove any control marker on that area and place one of your own. You can now push forward or end the push.
  3. Defenders Reveal: If some units or structures remain, the defending player must now move all units and defensive structures to the engaged area on the Map. Only defending workers that want to join the combat are revealed.
  4. Attackers Retreat: The attacking player can now decide to a) retreat with all attackers to any reachable areas through the area they came from, which ends the push, or b) commit to the engagement, resulting in combat. Retreating units do not spend a Movement Point on engaging the area.
  5. Combat: if the attackers commit, move any remaining structures and workers of the defending player to the area on the Map, and attackers spend a movement point moving into the area. Then perform a combat round.
Pushing Forward
Attackers that did not retreat, attack, or cast spells this round can push forward to a new area. Form a new Control Group from your current Control Group and follow the rules above.

Ending a Push
You can also end a push, allowing you to move any units in your current control group, that have not attacked or cast spells this round, to reachable areas you control. After ending a push, it becomes the next player in the current turn order to make a push or pass.
Special Rules
Cloaking: Can only be attacked and seen by players with a detector in the area.
Detect: Can see cloaked and burrowed units.
Flying: Can only be attacked by Air Attack.
Hover: Cannot be targeted by Spider Mines.
Static: Has speed 0 and cannot target enemies with a higher Range value.
Frenzy: Can attack again at the end of the combat if they survived and did attack this combat round.
Combat
First, reveal any new Attack, Armor or Speed upgrades.

Starting with units of the highest Range, also considering spells, perform a Combat Step wherein these units can attack or cast spells. In a Combat Step, the attacking player first assigns targets and declares spells being casted, followed by the defending players in the current turn order.

All damage and spells of a Combat Step take effect simultaneously before moving on the the next Combat Step. Remove any units that are killed and use tokens to show lost Health and Shield points. Units can decide not to attack in a Combat Step to make responsive attack in a later Combat Step.

Units can only attack once per round.

Workers that joined the combat can attack or repair but cannot collect resources next round.

Spell casters can only cast one spell with the icon per round, unless their Energy level is upgraded.

Assign Targets
Split the units that will attack in this Combat Step into groups by which unit type they will target. Spell casters then declares which spells they are casting and their targets.

Air units (and units dropped by transporting air units) can freely select their targets while ground units can only select targets that have the same or lower Range value. If there are no enemy units with the same or lower Range, ground units have to select targets in ascending order of Range. Spell-casters and non-defensive structures are always targeted last by ground units unless they have used spells in this Combat in which their Range value is equal to the lowest range of those spells. Workers in combat can be targeted normally while other workers are targeted last together with spell-casters.

Deal Damage
To determine the amount of damage a unit will deal to the enemy, calculate their Modified Attack Value (MAV). The attacker allocates the damage onto the targets.

MAV = Attack + Upgrades + Bonus - Armor

MAV can never be lower than 1, except for workers which always have MAV = Â½. Workers thus need to attack in pairs to deal any damage.

Attack: The Attack value shown on the race sheet as Gr. if the targets are ground units or Air if they are flying.
Upgrades: The amount of bonus attack coming from researched Attack upgrades.
Armor: The Armor value of the target units including Armor upgrades.
Bonus: Bonus from Explosive, Concussive, and Splash Damage, and Acid Spores.

Bonus
Explosive (e) attacks deal one additional damage to large units and one less to small.
Concussive (c) attacks deal an additional damage to small units and one less to large.
Acid Spore (a) attacks deal one additional damage per acid spore on the target and adds one acid spore to all targets that are dealt damage.
Splash (s) attacks deal 1/2/3 additional damage if there are 3/7/12 or more targets of the same type.
Suicide (!) attacks kills the attacker.
Winning
Elimination
Ways to get eliminated:
  • You loose all units
  • You loose all structures
  • You surrender
Winning
  • All other players are eliminated
  • After round 36, the player with the most areas under control wins. As long as it's a tie, play another round.
